BeatFlow Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

BeatFlow ("we", "our", "the app") is an offline music player. This policy explains what data we access and how we use it.

1. Data We Access

  • Audio files on your device — We read audio files stored on your device to display and play them. We do not upload or share these files.
  • Media metadata — Song titles, artist names, album art stored in your device's media store. Read-only, never transmitted.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect any personal information.
  • We do not have servers or user accounts.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not access the internet for any purpose except optional album art loading from your device's local media store.

3. Permissions We Request

  • READ_MEDIA_AUDIO (Android 13+) — To scan and play music files.
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (Android ≤12) — To scan and play music files.
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK — To keep music playing in the background and show a notification player.
  • WAKE_LOCK — To prevent the CPU from sleeping during audio playback.

4. Local Storage

BeatFlow stores your playlists, play history, and settings locally on your device using an encrypted local database. This data never leaves your device.

5. Third-Party Libraries

BeatFlow uses open-source libraries (just_audio, on_audio_query, hive) for audio playback and local storage. None of these libraries transmit any data to external servers.

6. Children's Privacy

BeatFlow is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy. Changes will be reflected with a new "Last updated" date.
